3D Face Reconstruction

SenseTime

While interning at SenseTime, one of China’s largest AI companies, I worked to develop a deep network to combine information from a collection of photos of one person and produce a pose-invariant, expression-invariant detailed 3D model of that individual. I designed a two-stage coarse-to-fine framework. With the inspiration from a Stanford paper that LSTM can be used to refine 3D models, I used VGG as feature extractor and LSTM for combining information. First-stage results already showed great improvements over using one 2D image, beating state-of-the-art in synthetic data, and giving a reconstructed 3D model that was identifiable as the person in question using real photos.

  • Date: Summer 2017
  • Category: Computer Vision, Deep Learning

Unsupervised Sentiment Lexicon Induction

Cornell

While on exchange at Cornell University, I started work on a sentiment lexicon induction project with Professor Claire Cardie. I built an unsupervised method to induce the sentiment of all Chinese words in a specific domain from a small set of human-annotated words. Using special features of Chinese, like characters and radicals, and features proved useful elsewhere, like generated word vectors, I have been able to obtain meaningful results.

  • Date: Spring 2017
  • Category: Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing

Sentiment and Market Prediction

HKUST

Based on the intuition that the sentiment of a given stock market report indicates market fluctuation, I worked with three other students under the supervision of Professor Qiang Yang to relate market reports to sentiment and further to stock market predictions. We used a convolutional neural network as the feature extractor for a given paragraph and fluctuations of the stock market as labels, and I was in charge of designing the network and coding. The resulting framework showed clear predictive power; once more features are added, the method will be a promising approach to market prediction.

  • Date: Fall 2016
  • Category: Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing
